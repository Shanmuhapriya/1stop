What is Vetiver?


Vetiver is a perennial bunchgrass of the family Poaceae, commonly known as Vetiver or Khus, whose roots are used for medicinal purpose. The word Vetiver in Tamil means "root that is dug up". It is found that it grows near water bodies. It plays a major role in Ayurvedic medcine treatment, also it reduces the body heat.

Benefits

  • Skin care:
               It is used for its antiseptic properties to treat acne and sores.
  • Crop protection and pest repellent:
                Vetiver's essential oil has anti-fungal properties against Rhizoctonia solani.
  • Vetiver as a termite repellent.
  • Animal feed:
                The leaves of vetiver are a useful byproduct to feed cattle, goats, sheep and horses.
  • Food and flavorings:
               Vetiver (khus) is also used as a flavoring agent, usually as khus syrup. Khus syrup is made by adding khus essence to sugar, water and citric acid syrup.
  • Perfumery and aromatherapy:
               Vetiver is mainly cultivated for the fragrant essential oil distilled from its roots.
  • Medicine:
               Vetiver has been used in traditional medicine in South Asia (India, Pakistan, Sri Lanka), Southeast Asia (Malaysia, Indonesia, Thailand), and West Africa.               Old Tamil literature mentions the use of vetiver for medical purposes.
